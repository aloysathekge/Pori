Asking the user a question: ask_user_input_v0

This renders tappable buttons instead of me typing "1) A or 2) B?" in prose — which is exactly what you saw when I asked about audience/length for the blog post. It's specifically for elicitation — gathering preferences/constraints before I can give useful help — not for general clarifying questions.

When I use it: ambiguous open-ended requests where the answer meaningfully changes what I'd produce (workout goals, book recommendations, blog post length/audience).

When I deliberately don't use it:





"A or B?" phrased questions — you want my opinion/analysis, not the same two options bounced back as buttons



Venting or emotional conversation — just respond, don't interrogate



You've already given me enough specifics — I proceed and state my assumption inline rather than re-asking

The rule of thumb: if I'm about to write clarifying questions as prose bullets ("Do you want X or Y? How long should it be?"), that's the signal to route through ask_user_input_v0  instead — buttons are just objectively less friction than typing free-text answers on mobile.

# AI Engineering Projects - Value Analysis

Looking at the 5 projects I listed, here's my ranking by **career value** (ROI for becoming a great applied AI engineer):

---

## 🥇 #1 Most Valuable: Fine-Tuned Classification Model with MLOps Pipeline

**Why it's worth the most:**
- ✅ **Highest demand skill** - MLOps is the #1 gap in most AI teams
- ✅ **Demonstrates end-to-end ownership** - training, deployment, monitoring
- ✅ **Directly transferable to any company** - every ML team needs this
- ✅ **Closes your biggest gap** - you lack model training/deployment experience
- ✅ **Interview gold** - you can walk through the full ML lifecycle

**Market value:** Companies hiring applied AI engineers prioritize candidates who can take models from notebook to production. This project proves you can.

---

## 🥈 #2: Multi-Agent System for Research & Writing

**Why it's valuable:**
- ✅ **Cutting-edge & differentiating** - agentic AI is where the field is heading
- ✅ **Leverages your unique strength** - you built Pori, double down on it
- ✅ **High visibility** - fewer people can build this well
- ✅ **Great for personal brand** - blog/talk material that stands out

**Trade-off:** More niche than MLOps, but positions you for high-growth areas (LLM agents, orchestration frameworks)

---

## 🥉 #3: Smart Document Q&A with Evaluation

**Why it's valuable:**
- ✅ **Builds on existing work** - fastest to ship
- ✅ **RAG is ubiquitous** - every company wants this
- ✅ **Shows engineering rigor** - evaluation is what separates good from mediocre

**Trade-off:** RAG systems are becoming commoditized. Less differentiation unless you do evaluation *really* well.

---

## #4: Real-Time Model Inference API

**Why it's less valuable (relatively):**
- ⚠️ **Specialized skill** - not every role needs low-latency optimization
- ⚠️ **Narrower applicability** - matters for high-scale production, less for early-stage
- ✅ **But demonstrates depth** - shows you can handle performance constraints

**Best for:** Roles at companies with scale (Google, Meta, trading firms)

---

## #5: Personal AI Coach/Advisor

**Why it's least valuable (for career):**
- ⚠️ **Broad but shallow** - touches many things but doesn't go deep on any
- ⚠️ **Product-focused** - less about ML engineering, more about UX/integration
- ✅ **Good portfolio piece** - demonstrates you can ship

**Best for:** If you want to start your own AI product company

---

## My Recommendation: Do These 2 in This Order

### **First: Project #2 (Fine-Tuning + MLOps)** - 4 weeks
This is your **foundation**. It fills your biggest skill gap and is universally valued.

### **Second: Project #3 (Multi-Agent System)** - 5 weeks  
This is your **differentiator**. It plays to your Pori experience and positions you uniquely.

**Total time investment:** ~9 weeks  
**Career impact:** You'll have both the **core ML engineering** credential AND a **unique specialty** that few candidates have.

---

## If You Only Have Time for ONE Project:

**Build Project #2 (Fine-Tuning + MLOps)**

It's the highest ROI because:
- It proves you can train and deploy models (table stakes)
- It's what 80% of applied AI job descriptions ask for
- It opens doors; the others help you stand out *after* you're in the door

The multi-agent work (#3) is your "advanced specialization" — do it once you have the fundamentals proven.

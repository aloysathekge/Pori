What makes it different

Four things most agents bolt on. Aloy builds them in.

A daily driver that's yours first — and grows into your team when you're ready, without becoming someone else's product.

Memory that's yours

Your notes, your preferences, your decisions — remembered and applied automatically. On a team, knowledge layers and the most specific wins: yours always overrides the team's, the team's overrides the org's.

Delegation, built in

Hand a subtask to an isolated sub-agent — one, several in parallel, or in the background. The heavy exploration stays in a throwaway context; you get the summary.

Transparent by default

Every run is a trace you can open — what it did, what it cost, why it stopped. You're never guessing what your agent just did with your name on it.

Personal and org, day one

Multi-tenant from the first line. A private agent that grows into an organization — teams, roles, shared knowledge — without a rewrite.

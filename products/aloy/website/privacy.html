Privacy Policy

Last updated: 10 July 2026

Aloy is a personal AI agent operated by Aloy Sathekge ("we", "us"). This policy explains what data Aloy collects, how it is used and stored, and the choices you have. The short version: your data powers your agent, and nothing else.

Data we collect

  • Account data — your email address and authentication identifiers, managed by our auth provider (Supabase).
  • Conversations — the messages you exchange with the agent, and the artifacts (files, plans, traces) its runs produce.
  • Memory — facts the agent stores to serve you across sessions (e.g. preferences, saved-file pointers). You can view and delete these in the app's Memory page.
  • Files — attachments you upload and files you save to "My Files", stored in our object storage.
  • Usage records — per-run token counts and cost accounting for your own usage page.

How we use it

Exclusively to operate Aloy for you: running your requests through AI models, remembering context you asked it to keep, and showing you your own history, files, and usage. We do not sell your data, use it for advertising, or train models on it.

Google user data

If you connect your Google account, Aloy accesses Google user data only to perform the actions you ask for:

  • Gmail — searching and reading messages when you ask the agent to work with your inbox; sending email only when you instruct it to.
  • Google Calendar — listing your events and creating events you request.

Message and event content retrieved this way is used in-the-moment to fulfil your request (including being sent to the AI model processing that request) and appears in your conversation history like any other agent output. We store your OAuth tokens encrypted at rest (Fernet/AES) and never your Google password. We do not transfer Google user data to third parties except as needed to process your request, and we do not use it for advertising.

Limited Use disclosure: Aloy's use and transfer of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

You can disconnect Google at any time from the app's Connections page, which deletes the stored tokens; you can also revoke access from your Google Account permissions.

Processors we rely on

  • AI model providers (Anthropic; Google; others you select) — receive the content of the requests your agent runs.
  • Supabase — authentication and database hosting.
  • Amazon Web Services — application hosting and encrypted secret storage (EU region).
  • Vercel — serves the web application.

Retention & deletion

Deleting a conversation deletes its messages, runs, traces, and stored files (files you explicitly saved to "My Files" remain until you remove them there). Deleting a memory entry removes it from future runs. To delete your account and all associated data, contact us at the address below.

Security

Data in transit is encrypted with TLS; OAuth tokens are encrypted at rest; secrets live in a managed KMS-backed store; per-tenant isolation is enforced on every data access path.

Contact

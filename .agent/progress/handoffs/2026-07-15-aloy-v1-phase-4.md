## Shipped in this phase

- `aloy_backend/proposal_executor.py` owns durable Proposal decisions and the
  non-agent executor.
- Decisions are tenant/Event scoped and compare-and-set from `pending`; edits
  cannot change the tool, are schema validated, and remain pending.
- Execution rechecks membership, organization policy, Event lifecycle, origin
  Agent tool access, live credentials/capabilities, the safe-tool allowlist,
  normalized args, and the stored schema fingerprint.
- `approved -> executing` is an atomic one-winner claim with a stable
  `execution_attempt_id`.
- Success stores provider operation evidence plus a `ToolExecutionReceipt`,
  moves to `committed`, and appends `proposal_committed` in the same transaction.
- Definite errors become `failed`; uncertain provider/receipt crash windows and
  stale execution claims become `indeterminate` and are not retried blindly.
- Expired pending Asks apply the wedge's reject safe default.
- The new Event decision endpoint and legacy conversation approval alias both
  feed the same rail; the worker polls approved Proposals as a durable fallback.
